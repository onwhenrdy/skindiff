# Chen, Han, Wittayanarakul, Hsieh (2018) — MLR + SVM Two-QSAR

## Citation
Chen, L., Han, L., Saib, O., Lian, G. (2013) and follow-up Chen et al.
(2018). Evaluating Molecular Properties Involved in Transport of Small
Molecules in Stratum Corneum: A Quantitative Structure-Activity Relationship
for Skin Permeability. Molecules 23(4):911. PMC: PMC6017021.
DOI: 10.3390/molecules23040911.

(Also: Chen, L., Han, L., Lian, G. (2013). Recent advances in predicting
skin permeability of hydrophilic solutes. Adv. Drug Deliv. Rev.
65:295-305. PubMed: 23022619.)

## Equation
Best multiple-linear-regression form (n=139 training):

log Kp = 2.068 - 6.515 * cos^2[(4.31 + ALOGP) / 8.66]
                - 0.722 * X3v - 0.168 * Neoplastic-80

where:
- ALOGP is the AlogP / Ghose-Crippen log P,
- X3v is the valence-corrected third-order Chi connectivity index,
- Neoplastic-80 is a Ghose-Viswanadhan-Wendoloski drug-likeness fragment
  count (binary-ish; non-trivial to compute).

Companion SVM model (no closed-form): R^2 = 0.946 train / 0.872 test on
n = 274 total.

## Training set
n = 274 compounds total; 139 training set, 135 test set. Compiled from the
literature with strict in-vitro / aqueous-donor inclusion criteria; supersets
the older Patel 158-compound set.

## Reported performance
MLR: R^2 = 0.901 on training, lower on test (paper does not headline a test
R^2 for MLR; they use SVM for the prediction-performance benchmark).
SVM: R^2 = 0.946 train, 0.872 test, RMSE in test ~ 0.34 log units.

## Validity / limitations
- The cos^2((4.31+ALOGP)/8.66) term is unusual and was chosen to capture a
  bilinear (or oscillatory) Kp-vs-ALOGP relationship; it has no
  physicochemical motivation, just a curve-fit. Predictions outside the
  training range of ALOGP are unreliable.
- Neoplastic-80 is a binary indicator that limits transferability outside
  drug-like compounds.
- Chen 2013 (the hydrophilic-solute paper) reported R^2 < 0.5 for QSPRs on
  hydrophilic compounds (log Kow < 0.5); mechanistic models did better there.

## Notes
Representative of the modern wave of Kp QSARs that use larger curated sets
and (often) machine-learning methods. The MLR form is reported alongside the
SVM as a transparent comparator. Use the SVM if predictive accuracy matters;
use the MLR if interpretability matters. Sister-agent material (D, K
predictions) often draws on this same dataset.
