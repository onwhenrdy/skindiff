# EDETOX database

## Description
EDETOX (Evaluation and Prediction of Dermal Absorption of Toxic Chemicals) was an EU 5th Framework project that ran 2000-2004, coordinated from Newcastle University. The project produced a database of in-vitro and in-vivo dermal absorption data for industrial / agrochemical compounds, intended as a resource for predictive QSAR development. Roughly 200 compounds with measured permeation data spanning multiple skin sources and vehicle conditions.

## Coverage
- ~200 compounds (verify exact count — varies between sources).
- In-vitro Franz-cell permeation data plus some in-vivo dermal absorption fractions.
- Mix of industrial chemicals, pesticides, and reference QSAR molecules.
- Includes vehicle composition where reported — one of the few datasets that does.
- Skin source labelled (human / pig / rat) where known.

## Access
Originally hosted at Newcastle University (research.ncl.ac.uk/edetox/). The website's current status is uncertain — the project ended over 20 years ago and academic project hosting often lapses. Verify accessibility before relying on it. The data tables are also available as appendices in the project's final report on the EU CORDIS database.

## Notes
- One of the few publicly available datasets with vehicle composition information.
- Compound list overlaps significantly with Flynn / Magnusson but adds occupational-exposure-relevant chemicals (industrial solvents, pesticide actives) under-represented in the older datasets.
- Has been used in several published QSARs, e.g. Wilkinson SC, Williams FM papers in mid-2000s.
- If the original hosting is unavailable, the data appears in supplementary materials of those papers.
