# Cosmetics Europe Long Range Science Strategy (LRSS) — dermal absorption datasets

## Description
Cosmetics Europe (the EU cosmetics-industry trade association) has, since the 2013 EU animal-testing ban, run a multi-year Long Range Science Strategy programme to generate validated in-vitro and in-silico methods for cosmetic safety assessment. The dermal absorption work has produced curated in-vitro permeation datasets for cosmetic ingredients that are systematically more representative of cosmetic use cases than the older Flynn / Magnusson sets (e.g. higher logP, formulation in non-aqueous vehicles, finite dose).

A subset of the data is published in the supplementary materials of LRSS-funded papers; some remains industry-internal but is summarized in workshop reports.

## Coverage
- ~50-150 cosmetic ingredients (verify exact count) with in-vitro Franz-cell permeation data.
- Vehicle conditions matched to cosmetic use (cream, lotion, leave-on / rinse-off).
- Multi-laboratory ring-trial validation for several reference compounds.
- Some compounds also have in-vivo human dermal absorption data (older studies pre-ban).

## Access
- Cosmetics Europe website hosts published reports and protocols: https://www.cosmeticseurope.eu/library/long-range-science-strategy/
- Published papers with supplementary data appear in Toxicol In Vitro, Reg Toxicol Pharmacol, ALTEX over the period 2014-2024.
- Some datasets remain restricted to LRSS partners.

## Citation
Cosmetics Europe LRSS workshop reports and individual publications. Key papers:
- Géniès C, Jamin EL, Debrauwer L, et al. "Comparison of the metabolism of 10 chemicals in human and pig skin explants." J Appl Toxicol 2019; 39: 235.
- Multiple Géniès / Cubberley / Westmoreland publications around 2015-2022.

## Notes
- One of the few sources of cosmetics-relevant Kp data with consistent experimental design across labs and compounds.
- Compound coverage skews to cosmetic actives (UV filters, preservatives, fragrances); less useful for pharmaceutical or industrial chemicals.
- Important counterweight to the Flynn-derived datasets that dominate published QSARs — Cosmetics Europe's compounds extend the physicochemical domain of applicability that QSARs can be validated against.
- Cosmetics Europe also funds development of related in silico tools through its NAMs Strategy programme; periodic ALTEX papers report progress.
