# Chen / Lian — review of QSPR plus mechanistic skin permeation models

## Citation
Specific reviews from the Chen-Lian-Han Surrey/Unilever group, e.g.:
- Chen LJ, Lian GP, Han L. "A mechanistic model for predicting human skin permeation." Skin Pharmacol Physiol 2010; 23: 168 (verify year).
- Chen LJ, Lian GP, Han L. Series of papers on the multi-pathway / brick-and-mortar SC model in J Pharm Sci, AIChE J around 2008-2018.
- Naegel A, Heisig M, Wittum G — independent line of microscopic SC modelling work (review-like papers).

## Coverage
The Chen-Lian-Han review papers and the Naegel-Heisig-Wittum line of work cover:
- The brick-and-mortar SC microstructure (corneocyte bricks of ~30-40 µm in lateral and ~0.5-1 µm in vertical dimensions, with ~0.1 µm intercellular lipid lamellae).
- Computational fluid dynamics / microscopic finite-element simulations of the lipid-pathway-only and lipid-plus-corneocyte permeation routes.
- How the microscopic D_lipid and D_corneocyte and partition coefficients relate to the macroscopic effective D_SC and K_SC.
- Comparison with empirical Kp QSARs and 1D macroscopic diffusion models.

## Key takeaways
1. For most compounds, the macroscopic 1D model (effective D_SC, K_SC) is indistinguishable from the microscopic brick-and-mortar model in terms of permeation prediction. The micro-model adds value only when one specifically asks about intracellular vs intercellular distribution or about the SC heterogeneity.
2. The corneocyte intracellular pathway contributes meaningfully for hydrophilic compounds (polar pathway) and for very large molecules where the lipid-only pathway is too narrow.
3. Predicted effective D_SC from the microscopic geometry agrees with measured tape-strip data to within a factor of ~3, which is good enough for risk-assessment-grade simulations.
4. The Surrey group's QSAR + microscopic-model hybrid is among the more physically grounded approaches but has not been packaged for general use.
